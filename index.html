Framework scripts — quick reference
"Let me think about it"
When people say that, what they usually mean is NO. You just don't want to hurt my feelings — isn't that the case here? Is it the money?
"I'll get back to you"
Usually when I hear that, I never hear from the person again. Let's bottom-line this — what would it take for us to do business today?
Price objection
Let's pretend I could offer you multiple payments — would that make a difference and we can break this up and go our separate ways smiling?
"Send more info"
I'll be happy to send it. But let me ask — if I send everything you asked for and you love what you see, are you ready to do business today?

Team Member Training Guide

Step-by-step instructions for team members with no customer service experience. Follow each stage precisely.

1
How to use the Response Generator (daily use)
+
Step 1: Copy the exact message your customer sent you on WhatsApp.

Step 2: Open the Response Generator tab. Paste the message in the box.

Step 3: Choose the response category. Ask yourself: "What is happening in this conversation?" — Is this a new contact? A price objection? A follow-up? Select accordingly.

Step 4: Add context if you have it — e.g. "Day 3 of conversation" or "They saw the seminar but didn't join."

Step 5: Click Generate. Read the response. If it feels right, copy and send it. If not, click Regenerate for a different version.

Golden rule: Never send a response without reading it first. You understand the customer — the AI gives you the best words.
2
How to nurture a brand new WhatsApp contact
+
Day 1 — The warmup: Never mention business or products on Day 1. Compliment something genuine about them — their status post, their business, their personality. Example: "I couldn't help but notice how professionally you carry yourself on here. Really encouraging."

Day 2 — Discovery: Ask about their work. Use open questions only: "What do you do?" / "How long have you been in that line?" / "Is business going the way you want?"

Day 3 — The bridge: Once you know their pain, connect it to MonieQuest naturally. Use the Pretend Close: "If I knew a system that could [solve their pain], would you be interested?"

Never: Pitch on the first message. Send product links before building trust. Use "please" or beg.
3
How to handle "I'll think about it" and other objections
+
When a customer gives resistance, go to the Objection Engine tab. Either paste what they said or click the matching objection chip. The AI will give you the exact counter-script.

The 3 rules of objection handling:
1. Never argue — always agree first
2. Always go to the root cause — "Is it the money?"
3. Use the Pretend Close — "If I could solve that, would you move forward today?"

If they still say no: Use the graceful exit — "No hard feelings, brother/sis. My mission is your success. Let's talk when the time is right."
4
How to transition a customer into a sale
+
The MonieQuest transition formula has 4 steps:

1. Pain → Possibility: "You mentioned you're struggling to get customers. What if I told you there's a system that makes customers come to you without begging?"

2. Commitment question: "If I am uniquely qualified to fix your pain, would you demonstrate the financial commitment to fix it?"

3. Value stack before price: Always reveal all the bonuses and value BEFORE mentioning price. Make the price feel like a steal.

4. Urgency + guarantee: "This price is only valid before [time]. And if you're not satisfied, I'll refund every kobo — no questions asked."
5
WhatsApp group seminar — team member role
+
Use the WhatsApp Seminar tab to get the correct message for each stage. Follow the stage order exactly.

Your role in the group:
• Enforce the rules — no links, no spam
• Pin important messages
• Send reminder messages 30 min before seminar starts
• DM everyone who didn't pay after the class using the follow-up template

Golden rule for seminars: The goal of the free class is to get them to pay for the masterclass. Every message should build towards that moment.
